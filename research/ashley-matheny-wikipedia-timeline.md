# Ashley M. Matheny - Timeline

## Early Life
- **Born**: West Virginia, small town along Ohio River
- **Childhood**: Water skiing, canoeing, camping along the river

## Education
- **Undergraduate**: The Ohio State University
  - BS in Civil Engineering
  - Began research on tree water use in Michigan forests
  
- **Graduate**: The Ohio State University
  - MS in Civil Engineering (2013)
  - PhD in Civil, Environmental, and Geodetic Engineering (2013-2016)
  - Advisor: Gil Bohrer
  - Dissertation: Plant hydrodynamic modeling for forest transpiration

- **Postdoctoral**: The Ohio State University (2016-2017)
  - Civil, Environmental, and Geodetic Engineering

## Career
- **2010**: U.S. Army Corps of Engineers
  - Studied water interactions with dams
  - Witnessed hydraulic jump on Ohio River (career-defining moment)

- **2017-present**: University of Texas at Austin
  - Assistant Professor, Jackson School of Geosciences
  - Department of Geological Sciences
  - Ecohydrology research program

## Research Milestones
- **2014**: Published species-specific transpiration responses in JGR Biogeosciences
- **2015**: Published stem water storage observations in Ecosphere
- **2016**: Published contrasting water acquisition strategies in Ecohydrology
- **2017**: Published calibration methods for tree water content sensors in JoVE
- **2018**: Developed LEAF logger system (published in HardwareX)
- **2021**: Co-authored plant hydraulics development in Noah-MP model (JAMES)
- **2021**: Contributed to SAPFLUXNET global database (Earth System Science Data)
